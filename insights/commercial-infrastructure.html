Commercial foundations / Essay 01

Why more commercial activity does not always produce more growth.

12 minute readStrategyCommercial systems

When growth slows, the natural response is to do more. Sometimes the more useful response is to look closely at what all that activity is being asked to carry.

The instinct to add volume

Revenue pressure demands visible action: more outbound, more advertising, more content, new software, another hire. Any of those may help. None can compensate for an offer the market does not understand, a promise buyers cannot verify or a team that learns very little from the responses it receives.

Activity makes whatever is already there more visible. When the foundations are strong, that is good news. When they are weak, it becomes expensive quickly.

Before asking how to generate more leads, ask whether the current approach deserves more volume.

What needs to sit beneath demand

We look at five parts of the commercial picture: strategic focus, competitive position, authority and evidence, joined-up execution, and the quality of decision-making. They do not have to be perfect, but they do have to make sense together.

1. Strategic focus

The company needs to know which market, problem and customer deserve its attention. Without that boundary, effort spreads thinly and every message becomes less specific.

2. Competitive position

Buyers need a clear reason to choose this company over another. Good positioning makes that reason easy to grasp without flattening everything distinctive about the offer.

3. Authority and evidence

Buyers check claims before they engage. The relevant proof needs to exist, and it needs to be easy to find in places they are likely to trust.

4. Joined-up execution

Outbound, content, partnerships and sales should reinforce the same idea. If each channel tells a different story, the buyer is left to make sense of the company alone.

5. Better judgement

Every response contains information. Good teams use it to change the audience, message, offer or process. Weak teams simply add it to a report.

A more useful place to begin

Instead of asking only, “How do we generate more leads?”, ask, “What is stopping the work we already do from producing more?” The answer may still be volume. But now it is a decision rather than a reflex.
