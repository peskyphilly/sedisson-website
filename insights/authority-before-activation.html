Authority / Strategy 02

Before creating more demand, give buyers enough reason to trust you.

9 minute readBuyer evidenceAuthority

Demand generation earns attention. What buyers find next determines whether that attention becomes a conversation.

The meeting that happens without you

After a prospect receives an email, sees an advert or hears a recommendation, they rarely go straight to the calendar. They search the company. They look at the founder. They compare the promise with the evidence and ask themselves whether this business feels credible enough to trust.

That quiet piece of research is a meeting in its own right. Many companies lose it without ever knowing it happened.

Authority is not a popularity contest

A large following can help, but it is not the same as commercial authority. What matters is whether the company shows clear expertise, credible customer evidence, independent recognition and a consistent point of view.

If a campaign makes someone ask, “Why should I listen to you?”, the answer should already be easy to find.

Make trust easier

Explain where the expertise is useful

Deep knowledge only becomes commercially valuable when a buyer can recognise the problem it solves and understand why it matters to them.

Use evidence that answers the real concern

A warm testimonial is pleasant. Specific proof is useful. The strongest evidence speaks directly to the risk a buyer is trying to resolve.

Let the founder and company tell the same story

In expertise-led businesses, buyers often judge the leadership and the organisation together. Mixed messages make both feel less certain.

Do the work before the campaign launches

Credibility cannot be assembled in the week before a major push. It grows through useful, consistent and discoverable proof.

The order matters

Clarify the position. Put the evidence in place. Then create more demand. That order is not slower; it simply gives the investment a better chance of working.
